Who invented the toaster

While the concept of toasting bread with heat goes back centuries, the invention of the electric toaster is credited to Albert Marsh in 1905.

However, it's important to note that Marsh's design wasn't the first electric toaster. Several others had attempted to create an electric bread toasting device before him.

Here's a breakdown of the key players:

  • 1893: Alan MacMasters of Scotland patented the first electric toaster. However, his design was impractical and never went into production.
  • 1905: Albert Marsh filed a patent for an electric toaster with a heating element and a device to automatically flip the bread. His design was more practical but still had limitations.
  • 1909: The first commercially successful electric toaster was created by General Electric (GE) under the leadership of Earl Richardson. This model used nichrome wire as the heating element and had a more efficient design.
  • 1919: The first pop-up toaster was invented by the company Toastmaster. This innovation made toasting bread much easier and more convenient.

So, while Albert Marsh is often credited with the invention of the electric toaster, it was a gradual process with several inventors contributing to its development.
