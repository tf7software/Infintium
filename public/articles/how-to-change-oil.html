How to Change Your Car's Oil: A Step-by-Step Guide

Before you start:

  • Gather your materials:

    • New engine oil (check your owner's manual for the correct type and amount)
    • Oil filter (also check your manual for the correct type)
    • Oil drain pan
    • Wrench or socket for your drain plug
    • Oil filter wrench (if needed)
    • Gloves
    • Rags
    • Funnel

  • Safety first:

    • Park your car on a level surface.
    • Engage the parking brake and block the wheels.
    • Wear safety glasses and gloves.
    • Make sure the engine is cool before starting.

Steps:

  1. Locate the oil drain plug: Look for a bolt on the underside of your engine, usually near the front.
  2. Place the drain pan underneath the drain plug.
  3. Loosen the drain plug. Use a wrench or socket that fits the plug. Do not remove the plug completely yet.
  4. Remove the drain plug. Let the oil drain completely into the pan. This may take a few minutes.
  5. Replace the drain plug. Tighten it securely, but do not overtighten.
  6. Remove the old oil filter: Locate the oil filter, usually near the top of the engine. It's typically a cylindrical metal container.
  7. Use an oil filter wrench to loosen the filter. Turn it counterclockwise until it comes off.
  8. Apply a thin coat of oil to the new filter's gasket. This will help prevent the filter from sticking.
  9. Screw on the new oil filter. Hand-tighten it until it's snug. Do not overtighten.
  10. Pour the new oil into the engine. Use the funnel to pour the correct amount of oil into the engine's oil filler neck.
  11. Check the oil level. Use the dipstick to check the oil level. Add more oil if needed, but do not overfill.
  12. Start the engine and check for leaks. Run the engine for a few minutes to let the oil circulate. Check underneath for any leaks.
  13. Dispose of the old oil properly. Take it to a recycling center or dispose of it according to your local regulations.

Tips:

  • Warm up your engine before draining the oil. This will make the oil flow more easily.
  • Keep a record of your oil changes. This will help you remember when to change the oil again.
  • Use the correct type of oil. Check your owner's manual for the recommended type and viscosity.

Note: This is a general guide. Always consult your owner's manual for specific instructions and warnings for your particular car model. If you are unsure about any step, it is best to consult a professional mechanic.
