Grass is green because of a pigment called chlorophyll.

Here's how it works:

  • Chlorophyll absorbs sunlight, primarily in the red and blue wavelengths.
  • Green light is reflected back, which is why we see grass as green.
  • This process of absorbing sunlight is essential for photosynthesis, where plants use sunlight to convert carbon dioxide and water into energy for growth.

So, in short, grass is green because of its ability to absorb most wavelengths of light except for green, which it reflects back to our eyes.
